Note
In POSIX systems, threads usually sleep on RTC or MONOTONIC clock. In Linux, threads sleep on MONOTONIC clock, which should fulfill most of the use cases. Rarely, we need thread to sleep on BOOTTIME clock, so that the sleep time can still be measured during suspended mode.
